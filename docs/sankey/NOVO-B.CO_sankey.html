Revenue Drivers

Novo Nordisk A/S operates primarily in two business segments: Diabetes and Obesity care and Rare disease. Over the past three years, the company has demonstrated significant growth, particularly in the Diabetes and Obesity care segment, which has consistently contributed the majority of total revenue.

In FY2023, the Diabetes and Obesity care segment generated $33.62 billion, accounting for approximately 92.6% of total revenue of $36.30 billion. This segment saw a substantial increase in FY2024, with revenue rising to $42.47 billion, representing a growth of 26.3% year-over-year. By FY2025, the segment further expanded to $45.24 billion, indicating a continued upward trajectory.

The Rare disease segment, while smaller, has also shown growth, increasing from $2.68 billion in FY2023 to $2.91 billion in FY2024, and reaching $3.06 billion in FY2025. This segment's contribution to total revenue remains modest, at around 6.4% in FY2025, but it reflects a steady increase that complements the core business.

The revenue mix has evolved significantly over the past three years, with the Diabetes and Obesity care segment becoming increasingly dominant. The overall total revenue grew from $36.30 billion in FY2023 to $51.23 billion in the trailing twelve months (TTM), showcasing the company's robust growth strategy and market demand for its products, particularly in diabetes management and obesity treatment.

Earnings & Profitability

Novo Nordisk's profitability metrics indicate strong operational efficiency. The gross margin has remained consistently high, with 84.6% in FY2023, 84.7% in FY2024, and a slight decrease to 81.0% in FY2025. The operating margin has also been impressive, standing at 44.2% for both FY2023 and FY2024, and slightly lower at 41.3% in FY2025.

The major cost drivers for Novo Nordisk include Research and Development (R&D) expenses, which have increased from $5.07 billion in FY2023 to $8.13 billion in FY2025, reflecting the company's commitment to innovation and product development. The R&D expense as a percentage of total revenue has also increased, indicating a strategic focus on expanding its product pipeline, particularly in diabetes and obesity care.

Operating leverage is evident in the company's financials, as operating income has grown from $16.03 billion in FY2023 to $19.95 billion in FY2025, despite fluctuations in gross margin. This growth in operating income, alongside a relatively stable operating margin, suggests that Novo Nordisk is effectively managing its costs while scaling its operations.

Business Model Summary

Novo Nordisk converts revenue into earnings through a well-structured business model that emphasizes high-margin products in the diabetes and obesity care markets. The company's ability to maintain high gross and operating margins reflects its competitive positioning and operational efficiency. The significant investment in R&D underscores its commitment to innovation, which is critical in the highly competitive pharmaceutical industry.

The segment structure reveals a clear focus on diabetes and obesity care, which not only drives the majority of revenue but also offers substantial growth potential. The gradual increase in the Rare disease segment, while still a smaller part of the overall business, indicates a strategic diversification that could enhance long-term sustainability.

Notably, the shift in revenue mix towards the Diabetes and Obesity care segment, coupled with a strong operating leverage, positions Novo Nordisk favorably in the market. The company's ability to adapt and grow in response to market demands, while maintaining profitability, highlights its robust business model and strategic foresight.
