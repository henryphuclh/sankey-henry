Revenue Drivers

Costco Wholesale Corporation operates primarily through five business segments: Food and Sundries, Non-Foods, Fresh Foods, Warehouse Ancillary and Other Businesses, and Membership. For the fiscal year 2025, total revenue reached $275.24 billion, with the following contributions from each segment:

  • Food and Sundries: $109.56 billion (39.8%)
  • Non-Foods: $71.19 billion (25.9%)
  • Warehouse Ancillary and Other Businesses: $51.17 billion (18.6%)
  • Fresh Foods: $37.99 billion (13.8%)
  • Membership: $5.32 billion (1.9%)

Over the past three years, Costco has seen a consistent increase in total revenue, growing from $242.29 billion in FY2023 to $275.24 billion in FY2025, representing a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of approximately 6.7%. The Food and Sundries segment has been a significant driver of this growth, increasing from $96.17 billion in FY2023 to $109.56 billion in FY2025, reflecting a 13.9% growth. The Membership segment also showed a positive trend, growing from $4.58 billion in FY2023 to $5.32 billion in FY2025, indicating a growing customer base and retention.

The revenue mix has evolved, with Food and Sundries becoming an increasingly dominant segment, while the Membership revenue, although smaller in absolute terms, is critical for Costco's business model as it provides a steady income stream that supports lower pricing strategies across other segments.

Earnings & Profitability

Costco's gross profit for FY2025 was $35.35 billion, yielding a gross margin of 12.8%. The operating income was $10.38 billion, resulting in an operating margin of 3.8%. The gross margin has shown a slight upward trend from 12.3% in FY2023 to 12.8% in FY2025, indicating improved efficiency in managing cost of goods sold (COGS) relative to revenue growth.

The major cost drivers for Costco include Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A) expenses, which totaled $24.97 billion in FY2025, and COGS, which is inherently linked to the volume of goods sold. Notably, Costco does not report R&D expenses, as its business model does not heavily rely on product innovation but rather on operational efficiency and scale.

Operating leverage is evident in Costco's profitability trends, as the company has managed to increase its operating income from $8.11 billion in FY2023 to $10.38 billion in FY2025, despite the relatively low operating margin. This suggests that Costco is effectively managing its fixed costs while scaling its operations, allowing for improved profitability as revenue grows.

Business Model Summary

Costco's business model is characterized by its ability to convert revenue into earnings through a membership-based warehouse retail format that emphasizes low prices and high volume. The company generates significant revenue from membership fees, which are crucial for maintaining its competitive pricing strategy. This model allows Costco to offer lower prices than traditional retailers, driving higher foot traffic and sales volume.

The competitive positioning of Costco is evident from its segment structure, with a strong focus on essential goods and a growing emphasis on fresh foods. The company’s ability to maintain a diverse product offering while keeping prices low has solidified its market position against competitors. The notable shift in revenue mix towards Food and Sundries and Fresh Foods indicates a strategic focus on essential and perishable goods, which are less price-sensitive and can drive higher customer loyalty.

In summary, Costco's business model leverages its membership structure, operational efficiency, and a strong focus on essential goods to drive revenue growth and profitability. The company’s ability to adapt its segment mix and maintain healthy margins positions it well for continued success in the competitive retail landscape.
