Revenue Drivers

Taiwan Semiconductor (TSM) operates primarily in the semiconductor manufacturing sector, with a significant focus on wafer fabrication. In FY2025, TSM reported total revenue of $121.91 billion, with the wafer segment contributing a substantial $104.74 billion, accounting for approximately 86% of total revenue. The "Others" segment, which includes various ancillary services and products, generated $17.17 billion, representing about 14% of total revenue.

Over the past three years, TSM's revenue has shown a remarkable upward trajectory, increasing from $70.46 billion in FY2023 to $121.91 billion in FY2025. This growth reflects a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of approximately 36.5%. The revenue mix has evolved, with the wafer segment consistently dominating total revenue, while the "Others" segment has remained relatively stable in its contribution.

Key products driving growth include advanced semiconductor nodes, particularly in the 5nm and 7nm technology processes, which are critical for high-performance computing and mobile applications. Geographically, TSM's customer base is diverse, with significant demand from North America and Asia, particularly from major technology firms that rely on TSM for cutting-edge chip manufacturing.

Earnings & Profitability

TSM's profitability metrics indicate strong operational efficiency. In FY2025, the company achieved a gross margin of 59.9% and an operating margin of 50.8%. These margins reflect the high value-added nature of semiconductor manufacturing, particularly in advanced nodes. The gross margin has shown a positive trend, increasing from 54.4% in FY2023 to 59.9% in FY2025, indicating improved cost management and pricing power.

Major cost drivers for TSM include research and development (R&D) expenses, which amounted to $7.89 billion in FY2025, representing about 6.5% of total revenue. This investment in R&D is crucial for maintaining technological leadership and developing next-generation semiconductor technologies. While specific selling, general, and administrative (SG&A) expenses are not disclosed, the absence of significant SG&A costs suggests a lean operational structure focused on manufacturing efficiency.

Operating leverage is evident in TSM's financials, as the company has been able to convert revenue growth into higher operating income. Operating income increased from $30.03 billion in FY2023 to $61.97 billion in FY2025, reflecting a significant improvement in operational efficiency and scale.

Business Model Summary

Taiwan Semiconductor effectively converts revenue into earnings through its high-margin wafer fabrication business, which benefits from economies of scale and advanced manufacturing capabilities. The company's competitive positioning is reinforced by its ability to serve a diverse range of customers across various industries, including consumer electronics, automotive, and telecommunications.

The segment structure highlights TSM's focus on wafer fabrication, which is the core of its business model. The notable increase in revenue and margins over the past three years indicates a successful strategy in capitalizing on the growing demand for semiconductors, particularly in advanced technology nodes.

Furthermore, the shift in revenue mix towards higher-margin products and the consistent investment in R&D underscore TSM's commitment to innovation and maintaining its leadership position in the semiconductor industry. The company's ability to adapt to market demands and technological advancements positions it favorably for continued growth in the evolving semiconductor landscape.
