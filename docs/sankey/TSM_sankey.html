Revenue Drivers

Taiwan Semiconductor (TSM) operates primarily in the semiconductor manufacturing sector, with its revenue largely derived from two main segments: Wafer and Others. In FY2025, TSM reported total revenue of $120.69 billion, with the Wafer segment contributing a substantial $103.69 billion, accounting for approximately 86% of total revenue. The Others segment generated $17.00 billion, representing about 14% of total revenue.

Over the past three years, TSM has experienced significant revenue growth. In FY2023, total revenue was $68.50 billion, which increased to $91.71 billion in FY2024, and further surged to $120.69 billion in FY2025. This represents a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of approximately 37.5% over the three-year period. The growth trajectory indicates a robust demand for semiconductor products, particularly in advanced technology nodes, which are critical for applications in high-performance computing, mobile devices, and automotive electronics.

The revenue mix has evolved, with the Wafer segment consistently dominating total revenue. The Others segment, while smaller, provides diversification and may include services such as design support and testing. The geographical distribution of revenue is not detailed in the provided data, but TSM's global customer base includes major technology firms, which likely contributes to its revenue stability and growth.

Earnings & Profitability

TSM's profitability metrics reflect strong operational efficiency. In FY2025, the company achieved a gross profit of $72.28 billion, resulting in a gross margin of 59.9%. This margin has shown an upward trend from 54.4% in FY2023 to 56.1% in FY2024, and further to 59.9% in FY2025. The improvement in gross margin can be attributed to the company's focus on advanced manufacturing processes and cost management strategies.

Operating income for FY2025 was reported at $61.35 billion, yielding an operating margin of 50.8%. This is a notable increase from 42.6% in FY2023 and 45.7% in FY2024, indicating that TSM has effectively leveraged its scale and operational efficiencies to enhance profitability. The major cost drivers include research and development (R&D) expenses, which amounted to $7.81 billion in FY2025, reflecting TSM's commitment to innovation and technology leadership. While SG&A expenses are not disclosed, they are typically a smaller component of total costs in manufacturing firms.

The significant increase in both gross and operating margins over the three years suggests that TSM is not only growing its top line but is also managing its cost structure effectively.
