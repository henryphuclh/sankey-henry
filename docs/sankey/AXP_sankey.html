American Express (AXP) Business Model Analysis

Revenue Drivers

American Express operates primarily through four business segments: U.S. Consumer Services, Commercial Services, International Card Services, and Global Merchant and Network Services. In FY2025, the total revenue reached $72.23 billion, with the U.S. Consumer Services segment contributing the largest share at $34.31 billion, accounting for approximately 47.6% of total revenue. This segment has shown consistent growth, increasing from $23.53 billion in FY2023 to $31.43 billion in FY2024, and further to $34.31 billion in FY2025.

The Commercial Services segment generated $16.93 billion in FY2025, slightly up from $15.86 billion in FY2024, indicating a stable revenue stream. International Card Services also saw growth, rising from $12.07 billion in FY2023 to $13.00 billion in FY2025. However, the Global Merchant and Network Services segment experienced a decline from $8.44 billion in FY2023 to $7.76 billion in FY2025, reflecting a shift in revenue mix.

The revenue mix has evolved over the past three years, with the U.S. Consumer Services segment becoming increasingly dominant. In FY2023, it represented 38.9% of total revenue, which increased to 47.6% by FY2025. This shift highlights the growing importance of consumer spending and loyalty programs in driving growth for American Express.

Earnings & Profitability

American Express has maintained strong profitability metrics, with gross margins consistently above 90%. In FY2025, the gross margin was 92.7%, up from 91.9% in FY2023. The operating margin also showed improvement, increasing from 17.4% in FY2023 to 19.1% in FY2025. This trend indicates effective cost management and operational efficiency.

The major cost drivers for American Express include Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A) expenses, which totaled $53.18 billion in FY2025, representing a significant portion of total expenses. Notably, R&D expenses are not disclosed, suggesting that American Express may focus more on customer acquisition and retention strategies rather than product development.

Operating leverage is evident in the company's financials, as revenue growth outpaced the increase in SG&A expenses. For instance, while total revenue grew from $60.52 billion in FY2023 to $72.23 billion in FY2025, SG&A expenses increased from $45.08 billion to $53.18 billion, indicating improved profitability.

Business Model Summary

American Express effectively converts revenue into earnings through its high-margin business model, characterized by a focus on premium customer segments and value-added services. The company generates revenue primarily from transaction fees, annual membership fees, and interest income from cardholders. The high gross margins reflect the premium nature of its offerings, which include exclusive rewards programs and travel benefits.

The competitive positioning of American Express is evident from its segment structure, with a strong emphasis on the U.S. Consumer Services segment. This focus allows the company to leverage brand loyalty and customer retention, which are critical in the competitive financial services landscape. The growth in this segment, alongside stable performance in Commercial Services, positions American Express favorably against competitors.

Notable shifts in the segment mix include the increasing dominance of U.S. Consumer Services, which has grown significantly over the past three years. This trend, coupled with the decline in Global Merchant and Network Services, suggests a strategic pivot towards consumer-centric offerings. Overall, American Express's business model is characterized by strong profitability, effective cost management, and a clear focus on high-value customer segments.
