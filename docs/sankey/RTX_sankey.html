Revenue Drivers

RTX Corporation operates through three primary business segments: Collins Aerospace Systems, Pratt and Whitney, and Raytheon. In FY2025, the total revenue reached $88.60 billion, with each segment contributing as follows:

  • Collins Aerospace Systems: $27.59 billion (31.2%)
  • Pratt and Whitney: $32.92 billion (37.2%)
  • Raytheon: $27.89 billion (31.6%)

Over the past three years, RTX has experienced significant revenue growth, increasing from $68.92 billion in FY2023 to $88.60 billion in FY2025, representing a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of approximately 27.5%. The revenue mix has evolved, with Pratt and Whitney showing the most substantial growth, increasing from $18.30 billion in FY2023 to $32.92 billion in FY2025. This segment's growth is driven by demand for advanced aerospace engines and services, particularly in the commercial aviation sector.

Collins Aerospace Systems has also seen growth, albeit at a slower pace, with revenue rising from $26.25 billion in FY2023 to $27.59 billion in FY2025. The segment's performance is bolstered by a diverse portfolio of aerospace systems and services, including avionics, landing systems, and cabin systems. Raytheon, while stable, has shown modest growth from $26.35 billion in FY2023 to $27.89 billion in FY2025, reflecting ongoing demand in defense and intelligence sectors.

Earnings & Profitability

RTX's gross margin has improved from 17.5% in FY2023 to 20.1% in FY2025, indicating enhanced efficiency and cost management across its operations. The operating margin has also increased from 5.2% in FY2023 to 10.5% in FY2025. This improvement in profitability can be attributed to several factors, including effective cost control measures and a favorable revenue mix.

The major cost drivers for RTX include Research and Development (R&D) and Selling, General & Administrative (SG&A) expenses. In FY2025, R&D expenses were $2.81 billion, while SG&A expenses were $6.09 billion. The company has maintained a consistent R&D investment, which is crucial for innovation and maintaining competitive advantages in the aerospace and defense sectors. The operating leverage is evident as revenue growth has outpaced the increase in operating expenses, leading to improved profitability.

Business Model Summary

RTX Corporation effectively converts revenue into earnings through a well-structured business model that leverages its diverse segment offerings. The company’s ability to generate significant gross and operating margins is indicative of its operational efficiency and strategic focus on high-margin products and services.

The competitive positioning of RTX is reinforced by its segment structure, which allows for cross-segment synergies and a comprehensive approach to customer needs in both commercial and defense markets. The notable shift in revenue contribution from Pratt and Whitney highlights the growing importance of this segment, driven by increased demand for advanced aerospace technologies.

Overall, RTX's business model demonstrates resilience and adaptability, with a clear focus on innovation and operational excellence. The company’s strategic investments in R&D and its ability to manage costs effectively position it well for continued growth and profitability in the evolving aerospace and defense landscape.
