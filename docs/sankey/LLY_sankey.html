Eli Lilly and Company (LLY) Business Model Analysis

Revenue Drivers

Eli Lilly and Company operates primarily through two segments: Net Product Revenue and Collaboration and Other Revenue. In FY2025, Net Product Revenue accounted for approximately 93.5% of total revenue, generating $60.96 billion, while Collaboration and Other Revenue contributed $4.22 billion. This trend has remained consistent over the past three years, with Net Product Revenue increasing from $28.81 billion in FY2023 to $60.96 billion in FY2025, reflecting a robust growth trajectory.

The revenue mix has evolved significantly, with total revenue increasing from $34.12 billion in FY2023 to $65.18 billion in FY2025, representing a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of approximately 56.5%. This growth has been driven by key products such as Mounjaro®, which saw a dramatic increase in revenue from $882 million in Q2 2023 to $6.52 billion in Q3 2025. Other notable contributors include the oncology segment, which generated $2.41 billion in Q3 2025, and established products like Verzenio® and Taltz®, which have also shown consistent revenue growth.

Earnings & Profitability

Eli Lilly's profitability metrics indicate strong operational efficiency. The gross margin has improved from 79.2% in FY2023 to 83.0% in FY2025, reflecting effective cost management and a favorable product mix. The operating margin has also seen a significant increase, rising from 30.3% in FY2023 to 45.6% in FY2025. This improvement can be attributed to the company's ability to leverage its growing revenue base while controlling costs.

Major cost drivers include Research and Development (R&D) and Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A) expenses. R&D expenses increased from $9.31 billion in FY2023 to $13.34 billion in FY2025, reflecting the company's commitment to innovation and pipeline development. SG&A expenses also rose from $7.40 billion to $11.09 billion over the same period, indicating increased investment in marketing and sales efforts to support new product launches.

The operating leverage is evident as the company has managed to increase its operating income from $10.33 billion in FY2023 to $29.70 billion in FY2025, showcasing a strong ability to convert revenue growth into profit.

Business Model Summary

Eli Lilly's business model effectively converts revenue into earnings through a combination of high gross margins and operational efficiency. The company's focus on innovative therapies, particularly in the oncology and diabetes segments, positions it competitively within the pharmaceutical industry. The significant growth in products like Mounjaro® and the consistent performance of established brands highlight the company's ability to adapt and thrive in a dynamic market.

The segment structure reveals a strong emphasis on proprietary products, which not only drive revenue but also enhance profitability due to their higher margins compared to collaboration revenues. The notable shift in revenue mix towards high-growth products indicates a strategic focus on areas with substantial market potential.

Overall, Eli Lilly's financial performance over the past three years demonstrates a robust business model characterized by strong revenue growth, improving margins, and effective cost management, positioning the company well for future growth in the pharmaceutical sector.
