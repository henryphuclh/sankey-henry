Revenue Drivers

Novartis AG operates primarily in the pharmaceutical sector, with a diverse portfolio of products that contribute significantly to its total revenue. For the fiscal year 2025, Novartis reported total revenue of $54.53 billion, with a gross profit of $42.98 billion, resulting in a gross margin of 78.8%. The company's revenue mix has evolved over the past three years, with notable contributions from key products.

The primary revenue drivers include:

  • Entresto: This heart failure medication generated $7.75 billion in FY2025, maintaining its position as a leading product.
  • Cosentyx: An immunology treatment, Cosentyx contributed $6.67 billion in FY2025, showing consistent growth from $4.98 billion in FY2023.
  • Kesimpta: This multiple sclerosis treatment has seen significant growth, with revenues increasing from $2.17 billion in FY2023 to $4.43 billion in FY2025.
  • Leqvio: A newer entrant, Leqvio's revenue rose from $355 million in FY2023 to $1.20 billion in FY2025, indicating strong market acceptance.

The revenue mix has shifted slightly, with the "Other" category, which includes various products and contract manufacturing, contributing $6.98 billion in FY2025, up from $5.43 billion in FY2023. This diversification helps mitigate risks associated with reliance on a few blockbuster drugs.

Earnings & Profitability

Novartis has demonstrated strong profitability metrics, with an operating income of $17.64 billion in FY2025, translating to an operating margin of 32.4%. The gross margin has remained robust, reflecting the company's ability to maintain pricing power and manage costs effectively.

Key cost drivers include:

  • Research and Development (R&D): Novartis invested $11.20 billion in R&D in FY2025, which is critical for sustaining its pipeline of innovative therapies.
  • Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A): SG&A expenses were $13.25 billion in FY2025, reflecting the costs associated with marketing and selling its products.

The operating leverage is evident as the company has managed to increase its operating income significantly, from $9.77 billion in FY2023 to $17.64 billion in FY2025, despite rising R&D and SG&A expenses. This trend indicates effective cost management and operational efficiency.

Business Model Summary

Novartis converts revenue into earnings through a well-structured business model that emphasizes innovation, strategic marketing, and operational efficiency. The company's competitive positioning is reinforced by its diverse product portfolio, which includes both established and emerging therapies across various therapeutic areas.

The segment structure reveals a balanced approach to revenue generation, with significant contributions from both established products like Entresto and Cosentyx, as well as newer products like Leqvio. The consistent growth in these segments indicates a strong market presence and the ability to adapt to changing healthcare needs.

Notably, the shift in revenue mix towards newer products and the "Other" category suggests a strategic focus on diversifying revenue streams, which can enhance resilience against market fluctuations. The overall margin profile has improved, with gross margins remaining above 75% over the past three years, indicating strong pricing power and cost control.

In summary, Novartis AG's business model is characterized by a strong emphasis on innovation, a diverse product portfolio, and effective cost management, positioning the company well for sustained growth and profitability in the competitive pharmaceutical landscape.
