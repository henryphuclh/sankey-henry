Revenue Drivers

NVIDIA Corp operates primarily through five business segments: Compute, Networking, Gaming, Professional Visualization, and Automotive. For FY2026, the total revenue reached $215.94 billion, with the Compute segment being the largest contributor at $162.36 billion, accounting for approximately 75% of total revenue. The Networking segment followed with $31.38 billion (about 14.5%), while Gaming generated $16.04 billion (around 7.4%). The Professional Visualization and Automotive segments contributed $3.19 billion and $2.35 billion, respectively.

Over the past three years, NVIDIA has experienced significant growth, particularly in the Compute segment, which saw a revenue increase from $102.20 billion in FY2025 to $162.36 billion in FY2026, reflecting a 59% year-over-year growth. The Networking segment also demonstrated robust growth, increasing from $12.99 billion in FY2025 to $31.38 billion in FY2026, a remarkable 142% increase. Conversely, the Gaming segment's revenue grew from $11.35 billion in FY2025 to $16.04 billion in FY2026, indicating a more modest growth rate of 41%.

Key products driving this growth include NVIDIA's GPUs, which are essential for AI, gaming, and data center applications. The company's focus on high-performance computing and AI technologies has positioned it favorably in rapidly expanding markets, particularly in data centers and cloud computing.

Earnings & Profitability

NVIDIA's profitability is reflected in its strong gross and operating margins. For FY2026, the gross margin was 71.1%, while the operating margin stood at 60.4%. The Compute segment, being the largest, also boasts a high gross margin, contributing significantly to overall profitability. The Networking segment, while smaller, has shown a gross margin that supports its rapid revenue growth.

Major cost drivers for NVIDIA include Research and Development (R&D) and Selling, General, and Administrative (SG&A) expenses. In FY2026, R&D expenses were $18.50 billion, representing about 8.6% of total revenue, while SG&A expenses were $4.58 billion (approximately 2.1% of total revenue). The company has maintained a disciplined approach to managing these costs, allowing for substantial operating leverage as revenue has increased.

The operating income for FY2026 was $130.39 billion, reflecting a significant increase from $81.45 billion in FY2025. This trend indicates that NVIDIA has effectively leveraged its revenue growth to enhance profitability, with operating margins remaining robust despite increased investment in R&D.

Business Model Summary

NVIDIA converts revenue into earnings through a combination of high-margin products and efficient cost management. The company's ability to maintain gross margins above 70% across its segments demonstrates its competitive advantage in the technology sector, particularly in high-performance computing and AI applications.

The segment structure reveals a strong competitive positioning, with the Compute segment leading in both revenue and profitability. The rapid growth in Networking and the steady performance of Gaming, Professional Visualization, and Automotive segments indicate a diversified revenue base that mitigates risks associated with reliance on a single market.

Notably, the shift in segment mix over the past three years highlights NVIDIA's strategic focus on high-growth areas such as AI and data centers, which are expected to continue driving future revenue. The increase in the Networking segment's contribution to total revenue from 10% in FY2025 to 14.5% in FY2026 illustrates this strategic pivot. Overall, NVIDIA's business model is characterized by its ability to innovate and adapt to market demands, ensuring sustained growth and profitability.
