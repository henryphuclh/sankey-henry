Earnings & Profitability

Siemens AG has maintained solid profitability metrics, with a gross profit of $35.68 billion in both FY2025 and FY2026, resulting in a gross margin of 38.5%. This margin reflects a slight decline from FY2024, where the gross margin was 39.3% with a gross profit of $33.21 billion. The operating income has remained stable at $10.67 billion for FY2025 and FY2026, translating to an operating margin of 11.5%. This is a decrease from FY2024's operating margin of 12.5%, indicating potential pressures on operating efficiency or increased costs.

The major cost drivers for Siemens include research and development (R&D) expenses, which amounted to $7.70 billion in FY2026, and selling, general, and administrative (SG&A) expenses, which were $17.40 billion. The R&D expense has seen a consistent increase, reflecting Siemens' commitment to innovation and technology development, while SG&A expenses have also risen, indicating a focus on expanding operational capabilities and market reach.

Operating leverage appears to be a critical factor in Siemens' profitability trends. Despite stable revenues, the slight decline in operating margin suggests that the company may be facing challenges in managing its cost structure effectively. However, the consistent gross profit levels indicate that Siemens is maintaining its pricing power and operational efficiency in its core business areas.

However, the company must remain vigilant in managing its cost structure and enhancing operational efficiencies to sustain its profitability in a competitive landscape. Overall, Siemens AG's business model is characterized by a strong focus on innovation, a diverse product portfolio, and a commitment to maintaining profitability through effective cost management.
