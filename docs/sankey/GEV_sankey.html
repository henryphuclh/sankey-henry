Revenue Drivers

GE Vernova Inc. (GEV) operates primarily in three business segments: Power, Wind, and Electrification. For the fiscal year 2025, GEV reported total revenue of $38.07 billion, with the following contributions from each segment:

  • Power: $19.77 billion (52% of total revenue)
  • Wind: $9.11 billion (24% of total revenue)
  • Electrification: $9.64 billion (25% of total revenue)

Over the past three years, GEV has experienced a notable evolution in its revenue mix. In FY2024, total revenue was $34.94 billion, with the Power segment contributing $18.13 billion, Wind at $9.70 billion, and Electrification at $7.55 billion. This indicates a growth in total revenue from FY2024 to FY2025, primarily driven by the Power segment, which increased its revenue by $1.64 billion.

Key products and services driving growth include advanced power generation technologies, wind turbine solutions, and electrification products that cater to the growing demand for renewable energy and energy efficiency. The Power segment remains the largest revenue driver, reflecting GEV's strong position in traditional and renewable energy markets.

Earnings & Profitability

In terms of profitability, GEV reported a gross profit of $7.54 billion for FY2025, resulting in a gross margin of 19.8%. The operating income for the same period was $1.39 billion, yielding an operating margin of 3.6%. This represents an improvement from FY2024, where gross profit was $6.08 billion and the gross margin was 17.4%, indicating a positive trend in profitability.

The major cost drivers for GEV include Research and Development (R&D) expenses, which amounted to $1.20 billion in FY2025, and Selling, General, and Administrative (SG&A) expenses, which were $4.95 billion. The company has maintained a focus on R&D to innovate and enhance its product offerings, particularly in the electrification and renewable energy sectors.

Operating leverage appears to be improving, as evidenced by the increase in operating income from $471 million in FY2024 to $1.39 billion in FY2025, despite the relatively modest growth in total revenue. This suggests that GEV is effectively managing its cost structure while capitalizing on revenue growth.

Business Model Summary

GE Vernova Inc. converts revenue into earnings through a combination of efficient cost management and a diversified product portfolio that addresses the evolving energy landscape. The company's competitive positioning is evident from its segment structure, with a strong emphasis on Power, which remains the largest contributor to revenue. The Wind and Electrification segments also play crucial roles in GEV's strategy to capture market share in renewable energy and energy efficiency solutions.

Notable shifts in the segment mix are observed, particularly in the Power segment, which has shown consistent growth, while the Wind segment experienced a decline from $9.70 billion in FY2024 to $9.11 billion in FY2025. This shift may indicate a strategic pivot or market dynamics affecting wind energy solutions. The Electrification segment, however, has shown robust growth, increasing from $7.55 billion in FY2024 to $9.64 billion in FY2025, highlighting GEV's commitment to innovation in energy-efficient technologies.

Overall, GEV's business model reflects a strong alignment with global energy trends, focusing on sustainable solutions while maintaining a solid financial foundation through effective cost management and strategic investments in R&D.
