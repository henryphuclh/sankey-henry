Goldman Sachs Group Inc. Business Model Analysis

Revenue Drivers

Goldman Sachs Group Inc. operates primarily through three business segments: Global Banking & Markets, Asset & Wealth Management, and Platform Solutions. In FY2025, the total revenue reached $58.28 billion, with Global Banking & Markets contributing the largest share at $41.45 billion, accounting for approximately 71% of total revenue. Asset & Wealth Management followed with $16.68 billion (29%), while Platform Solutions generated $1.38 billion (2%).

Over the past three years, the revenue mix has evolved significantly. In FY2023, Global Banking & Markets generated $30.00 billion, which represented 65% of total revenue, while Asset & Wealth Management contributed $13.88 billion (30%) and Platform Solutions $3.42 billion (5%). By FY2024, Global Banking & Markets increased to $34.94 billion (65%), and Asset & Wealth Management rose to $16.14 billion (30%), but Platform Solutions saw a decline to $2.43 billion (5%). This trend continued into FY2025, where Global Banking & Markets further increased its dominance, reflecting a strategic focus on this segment.

Key products driving growth include investment banking services, trading and market-making activities, and wealth management solutions. Geographically, Goldman Sachs has a strong presence in North America, which remains a significant contributor to its revenue, although it also has a growing footprint in international markets.

Earnings & Profitability

Goldman Sachs has demonstrated strong profitability metrics, with gross margins consistently above 97% across the last three fiscal years. In FY2025, the gross margin was 98.1%, reflecting the high-value nature of its services. The operating margin also showed a positive trend, increasing from 23.2% in FY2023 to 37.5% in FY2025, indicating improved operational efficiency and cost management.

The major cost drivers for Goldman Sachs include Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A) expenses, which totaled $37.54 billion in FY2025, up from $34.49 billion in FY2023. Notably, there are no reported R&D expenses, which is typical for financial services firms. The increase in SG&A expenses has been offset by revenue growth, leading to enhanced operating leverage. The operating income rose from $10.74 billion in FY2023 to $21.85 billion in FY2025, showcasing the company's ability to convert revenue into earnings effectively.

Business Model Summary

Goldman Sachs converts revenue into earnings primarily through its high-margin business model, which leverages its expertise in investment banking, trading, and wealth management. The company’s competitive positioning is evident from its segment structure, with Global Banking & Markets being the cornerstone of its operations. This segment not only generates the majority of revenue but also exhibits the highest margins, underscoring its critical role in the overall business model.

The notable shift in segment mix over the past three years highlights a strategic pivot towards enhancing the Global Banking & Markets segment, which has seen substantial revenue growth from $30.00 billion in FY2023 to $41.45 billion in FY2025. Conversely, the Platform Solutions segment has experienced a decline, indicating a potential area for reassessment or strategic realignment.

Overall, Goldman Sachs' business model is characterized by its ability to maintain high gross and operating margins while effectively managing costs. The firm’s focus on its core competencies in banking and markets positions it well for sustained profitability and growth in the competitive financial services landscape.
