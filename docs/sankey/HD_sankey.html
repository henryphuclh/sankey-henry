Revenue Drivers

Home Depot Inc. (HD) generates its revenue primarily through a diverse range of segments, with total revenue reaching $164.68 billion in FY2026. The key segments contributing to this revenue include:

  • Outdoor Garden: $20.64 billion
  • Appliances: $13.99 billion
  • Power: $13.17 billion
  • Plumbing: $12.48 billion
  • Building Materials: $12.38 billion
  • Lumber: $11.39 billion
  • Paint: $10.98 billion

Over the past three years, the revenue mix has shown some shifts. For instance, the Outdoor Garden segment peaked at $25.02 billion in FY2024 but declined to $20.64 billion in FY2026. Conversely, Appliances and Power segments have remained relatively stable, with Appliances generating $14.05 billion in FY2025 and slightly decreasing to $13.99 billion in FY2026. The Plumbing segment also showed resilience, contributing $12.36 billion in FY2025 and $12.48 billion in FY2026.

The revenue mix indicates a strong reliance on home improvement and seasonal products, with the Outdoor Garden segment being a significant driver, particularly in FY2024. The company’s geographic reach across North America allows it to capitalize on various market demands, contributing to its overall revenue stability.

Earnings & Profitability

Home Depot's profitability is reflected in its gross margin and operating margin, which have remained relatively stable over the past three years. The gross margin for FY2026 was 33.3%, slightly down from 33.4% in FY2025 and FY2024. The operating margin for FY2026 was 12.7%, a decrease from 13.5% in FY2025 and 14.2% in FY2024.

The major cost drivers for Home Depot include Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A) expenses, which amounted to $30.70 billion in FY2026, up from $28.75 billion in FY2025. The increase in SG&A reflects the company's investment in customer service and operational efficiency. Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) is also a significant factor, impacting gross margins, although specific figures for COGS are not provided.

Home Depot has demonstrated operating leverage, as evidenced by its ability to maintain profitability despite fluctuations in revenue. The operating income for FY2026 was $20.89 billion, down from $21.53 billion in FY2025, indicating a slight contraction in profitability amidst rising costs.

Business Model Summary

Home Depot effectively converts revenue into earnings through a combination of strong sales across its diverse product segments and efficient cost management. The company’s gross profit for FY2026 was $54.87 billion, translating to a net income of $14.16 billion. This conversion reflects the company's ability to maintain a healthy margin profile despite competitive pressures.

The competitive positioning of Home Depot is evident from its segment structure, which allows it to cater to a wide range of customer needs, from DIY enthusiasts to professional contractors. Notable shifts in segment mix include the decline in the Outdoor Garden segment from its peak in FY2024, which may indicate changing consumer preferences or seasonal impacts. Additionally, the stability in Appliances and Plumbing suggests these segments are core to Home Depot's long-term strategy.
