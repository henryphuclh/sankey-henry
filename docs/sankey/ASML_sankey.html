Revenue Drivers

ASML Holding NV operates primarily in the semiconductor equipment industry, focusing on the design and manufacturing of photolithography systems used in the production of integrated circuits. The company's revenue is derived from several key segments, with the most significant contributions coming from Net service and field option sales, NXE systems, and ArF immersion systems.

In FY2025, ASML reported total revenue of $32.67 billion, with the following contributions from its primary segments:

  • Net service and field option sales: $8.19 billion (25.1%)
  • NXE systems: $10.45 billion (32.0%)
  • ArF immersion systems: $10.31 billion (31.6%)

Over the past three years, the revenue mix has evolved, with a notable increase in the contribution from Net service and field option sales, which grew from $5.62 billion in FY2023 to $8.19 billion in FY2025. Conversely, the revenue from NXE systems saw fluctuations, peaking at $10.45 billion in FY2025 after a dip in FY2024. The ArF immersion segment also maintained a strong presence, contributing significantly to overall revenue.

The growth in revenue can be attributed to the increasing demand for advanced semiconductor manufacturing technologies, particularly in regions such as Asia, where major semiconductor manufacturers are ramping up production capabilities.

Earnings & Profitability

ASML's profitability is reflected in its gross and operating margins, which have remained robust over the past three years. In FY2025, the company achieved a gross margin of 52.8% and an operating margin of 34.6%. These margins indicate strong pricing power and operational efficiency within the semiconductor equipment sector.

The major cost drivers for ASML include Research and Development (R&D) and Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A) expenses. In FY2025, R&D expenses amounted to $4.70 billion, while SG&A expenses were $1.26 billion. The company has consistently invested in R&D to maintain its technological edge, with R&D expenses increasing from $3.98 billion in FY2023 to $4.70 billion in FY2025. This investment is crucial for developing next-generation lithography systems, which are essential for meeting the evolving demands of semiconductor manufacturers.

Operating leverage is evident in ASML's profitability trends, as the company has been able to increase its operating income from $9.04 billion in FY2023 to $11.30 billion in FY2025, despite fluctuations in total revenue. This trend suggests that ASML is effectively managing its cost structure while capitalizing on revenue growth.

Business Model Summary

ASML's business model is characterized by its ability to convert revenue into earnings through a combination of high-margin products and services, strategic investments in R&D, and operational efficiencies. The company's competitive positioning is reinforced by its diverse segment structure, which allows it to cater to various customer needs within the semiconductor industry.

The notable shifts in segment mix include the increasing importance of Net service and field option sales, which have become a significant revenue driver, reflecting the growing trend of customers seeking ongoing support and services post-purchase. Additionally, the EXE segment, which generated $1.16 billion in FY2025, indicates ASML's focus on expanding its offerings in extreme ultraviolet (EUV) lithography technology.

Overall, ASML's strong financial performance, driven by its innovative product offerings and strategic focus on R&D, positions the company favorably within the competitive landscape of semiconductor manufacturing equipment. The consistent growth in revenue and profitability underscores the effectiveness of its business model in adapting to the dynamic demands of the semiconductor market.
