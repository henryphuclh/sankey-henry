Revenue Drivers

Intuit Inc. operates through several key business segments, which have shown significant growth and diversification over the past three years. The primary segments include:

  1. Global Business Solutions: This segment generated $11.08 billion in FY2025, accounting for approximately 58.8% of total revenue. It has consistently been the largest contributor, reflecting Intuit's strong position in providing solutions for small businesses and self-employed individuals.

  2. Consumer: This segment contributed $4.87 billion in FY2025, representing about 24.2% of total revenue. The Consumer segment has shown steady growth, increasing from $4.13 billion in FY2023 to $4.87 billion in FY2025.

  3. Credit Karma: Revenue from Credit Karma reached $2.26 billion in FY2025, up from $1.63 billion in FY2023. This segment has become a significant growth driver, reflecting Intuit's successful integration of financial services.

  4. ProTax: This segment generated $621 million in FY2025, showing modest growth from $561 million in FY2023. While it remains the smallest segment, it contributes to the overall diversification of Intuit's revenue streams.

The revenue mix has evolved, with Global Business Solutions maintaining its dominance while Consumer and Credit Karma segments have shown robust growth. The overall total revenue increased from $14.37 billion in FY2023 to $20.12 billion on a trailing twelve-month (TTM) basis, indicating a strong upward trajectory.

Earnings & Profitability

Intuit's profitability metrics reflect its efficient operations and strong market position. The gross margin for FY2025 was 80.4%, slightly improving from 79.3% in FY2023. This high gross margin is indicative of the company's ability to maintain pricing power and operational efficiency across its segments.

Operating income for FY2025 was $4.92 billion, resulting in an operating margin of 26.1%, up from 21.9% in FY2023. This improvement in operating margin suggests effective cost management and operational leverage, particularly in the Global Business Solutions segment, which benefits from economies of scale.

Key cost drivers include:

  • Research and Development (R&D): R&D expenses increased from $2.54 billion in FY2023 to $2.93 billion in FY2025, reflecting Intuit's commitment to innovation and product development.
  • Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A): SG&A expenses rose from $5.06 billion in FY2023 to $6.64 billion in FY2025, indicating increased investment in marketing and sales efforts to drive growth.

The combination of high gross margins and controlled operating expenses has allowed Intuit to enhance its profitability over the past three years.

Business Model Summary

Intuit's business model effectively converts revenue into earnings through a combination of high-margin products and services, operational efficiency, and strategic investments in growth areas. The company leverages its strong brand and customer loyalty to maintain pricing power, particularly in its Global Business Solutions and Consumer segments.

The competitive positioning of Intuit is evident from its diverse segment structure, which allows it to mitigate risks associated with reliance on a single revenue source. The growth of the Credit Karma segment highlights Intuit's ability to adapt and expand into new financial services, enhancing its overall value proposition.

Notable shifts in segment mix include the increasing contribution of Credit Karma to total revenue, which underscores the importance of digital financial services in Intuit's growth strategy. Additionally, the improvement in operating margins indicates that Intuit is not only growing its top line but also enhancing its profitability through effective cost management and operational efficiencies.

In summary, Intuit Inc. has demonstrated a robust business model characterized by strong revenue growth, high profitability, and a strategic focus on innovation and customer-centric solutions. The company's ability to adapt to changing market dynamics and invest in high-growth areas positions it well for continued success in the future.
