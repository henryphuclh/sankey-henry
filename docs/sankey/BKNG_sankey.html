Revenue Drivers

Booking Holdings Inc. primarily operates in the Online Accommodation Reservation Services segment, which has shown significant growth over the past three years. In FY2025, this segment generated $23.97 billion, accounting for approximately 89.2% of the total revenue of $26.92 billion. This represents a notable increase from $21.00 billion in FY2024 and $19.68 billion in FY2023, indicating a robust upward trend in this core business area.

The revenue mix has evolved, with the Online Accommodation Reservation Services segment consistently being the dominant contributor. The company has also reported revenues from reclassifications, which amounted to $1.68 billion in FY2023, but this is not a primary revenue driver. The growth in the accommodation segment can be attributed to increased travel demand and the expansion of Booking Holdings' global reach, particularly outside the U.S.

Geographically, the revenue from markets outside the U.S. has shown variability but remains a critical growth area. For instance, in 2024Q3, revenue from outside the U.S. reached $3.83 billion, reflecting the company's successful international strategy. The Netherlands also contributed significantly, with revenues peaking at $4.16 billion in 2024Q3.

Earnings & Profitability

Booking Holdings has demonstrated strong profitability metrics, with operating income increasing from $5.83 billion in FY2023 to $8.82 billion in FY2025. The operating margin has also improved, rising from 27.3% in FY2023 to 32.8% in FY2025. This trend indicates effective cost management and operational efficiency, particularly in the Online Accommodation Reservation Services segment.

While gross profit figures are not disclosed, the operating income and margin percentages suggest that the company maintains a healthy profitability profile. Major cost drivers include Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A) expenses, which totaled $9.04 billion in FY2025, up from $8.31 billion in FY2024 and $8.33 billion in FY2023. The increase in SG&A expenses is likely associated with marketing efforts and operational scaling to support revenue growth.

The company has not reported R&D expenses, indicating that its primary focus is on marketing and customer acquisition rather than product development. This strategic choice aligns with its business model, which relies heavily on digital platforms for service delivery.

Business Model Summary

Booking Holdings Inc. effectively converts revenue into earnings through its streamlined operations and focus on the Online Accommodation Reservation Services segment. The company’s ability to generate significant operating income, coupled with improving margins, highlights its operational leverage. The transition from $5.83 billion in operating income in FY2023 to $8.82 billion in FY2025 illustrates the company's capacity to scale its operations efficiently.

The competitive positioning of Booking Holdings is evident from its segment structure, which is heavily weighted towards accommodation services. This focus allows the company to leverage its brand strength and extensive network of partnerships with hotels and other accommodation providers. The notable shift in segment mix, with a clear emphasis on online reservations, positions the company favorably in a growing market driven by increasing travel demand.

Overall, Booking Holdings Inc. has demonstrated a strong business model characterized by significant revenue growth, improving profitability, and a focused approach to its core segment. The company's strategic emphasis on online accommodation services, combined with effective cost management, positions it well for continued success in the evolving travel and hospitality landscape.
