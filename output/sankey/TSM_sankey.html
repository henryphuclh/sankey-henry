Revenue Drivers

Taiwan Semiconductor (TSM) operates primarily through two significant segments: Wafer and Others. In FY2025, the Wafer segment generated a substantial revenue of $3,272.55 billion, accounting for approximately 86.5% of the total revenue. The Others segment contributed $536.50 billion, representing about 13.5% of the total revenue. This clear dominance of the Wafer segment highlights TSM's focus on semiconductor manufacturing, which is critical for various applications across industries.

Over the past three years, TSM has shown a consistent upward trend in revenue from its key customer segments. Notably, revenue from Customer A [member] increased from $787.70 billion in FY2023 to $976.62 billion in FY2024, marking a growth of approximately 24%. This trend indicates a strong reliance on major customers, particularly in the semiconductor space, which is essential for TSM's growth trajectory. The Others segment, which includes various non-wafer related revenues, is also expected to contribute positively, as evidenced by its projected revenue in FY2025.

The revenue mix has evolved significantly, with the Wafer segment remaining the cornerstone of TSM's business model. The company’s focus on advanced technology nodes and high-performance chips has positioned it favorably in the market, catering to the growing demand for semiconductors in sectors such as automotive, consumer electronics, and data centers.

Earnings & Profitability

In terms of profitability, TSM has demonstrated robust gross profit figures over the last three years. The gross profit for FY2025 is projected at $2,281.29 billion, which translates to a gross margin of approximately 69.5% based on the Wafer segment's revenue. In FY2024, the gross profit was $1,624.35 billion, yielding a gross margin of about 66.5%. The increase in gross margin reflects TSM's operational efficiency and its ability to manage costs effectively, particularly in the Wafer segment.

The major cost drivers for TSM include Research and Development (R&D) and Cost of Goods Sold (COGS). R&D expenses have increased from $182.37 billion in FY2023 to $246.43 billion in FY2025, indicating TSM's commitment to innovation and technology advancement. This investment is crucial for maintaining its competitive edge in the semiconductor industry. However, the absence of SG&A expense data limits a comprehensive analysis of total operating costs.

Operating leverage appears favorable, as TSM's net income has grown significantly from $851.03 billion in FY2023 to $1,695.12 billion in FY2025, reflecting a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of approximately 66%. This growth in net income, alongside rising gross profits, suggests that TSM is effectively converting revenue into earnings, benefiting from economies of scale as production volumes increase.

Business Model Summary

Taiwan Semiconductor's business model is characterized by its ability to convert substantial revenue from its Wafer segment into significant earnings. The company's focus on high-margin products and advanced manufacturing processes allows it to maintain a strong profitability profile. The competitive positioning is evident from the segment structure, with the Wafer segment dominating revenue generation and showcasing TSM's leadership in semiconductor manufacturing.

Notable shifts in the segment mix are observed with the increasing contribution from major customers, particularly Customer A [member], which has shown a consistent upward trend in revenue. This reliance on key customers underscores the importance of maintaining strong relationships and delivering high-quality products to sustain growth.

Overall, TSM's business model is robust, driven by a strong revenue base in the Wafer segment, effective cost management, and a commitment to innovation through R&D investments. The company's ability to adapt to market demands and leverage its technological capabilities positions it well for future growth in the dynamic semiconductor landscape.
