Revenue Drivers

GE Vernova Inc. (GEV) operates primarily in three business segments: Power, Wind, and Electrification. For the fiscal year 2025, GEV reported total revenue of $38.07 billion, with the following contributions from each segment:

  • Power: $19.77 billion (51.9%)
  • Wind: $9.11 billion (23.9%)
  • Electrification: $9.64 billion (24.2%)

Over the past three years, the revenue mix has shown a notable shift. In FY2024, total revenue was $34.94 billion, with the Power segment contributing $18.13 billion, Wind at $9.70 billion, and Electrification at $7.55 billion. This indicates a growth trajectory for the Power segment, which increased by $1.64 billion year-over-year, while Wind saw a decline of $0.59 billion, and Electrification grew by $2.09 billion.

The key products driving growth include advanced power generation technologies, wind turbine systems, and electrification solutions that cater to a growing demand for renewable energy and energy efficiency. The geographical focus appears to be on markets with increasing energy needs and regulatory support for clean energy initiatives.

Earnings & Profitability

In FY2025, GEV achieved a gross profit of $7.54 billion, resulting in a gross margin of 19.8%. The operating income was $1.39 billion, translating to an operating margin of 3.6%. In comparison, FY2024 saw a gross profit of $6.08 billion and an operating income of $471 million, with gross and operating margins of 17.4% and 1.3%, respectively. This improvement in margins indicates a positive trend in operational efficiency and cost management.

The major cost drivers for GEV include Research and Development (R&D) expenses, which amounted to $1.20 billion in FY2025, and Selling, General, and Administrative (SG&A) expenses of $4.95 billion. The increase in R&D spending reflects GEV's commitment to innovation and maintaining a competitive edge in the energy sector. The SG&A expenses have also risen, which could be attributed to increased marketing and administrative costs associated with expanding operations.

Operating leverage is evident as GEV's revenue growth outpaced the increase in operating expenses, leading to improved profitability. The company’s ability to convert revenue into earnings is supported by its focus on high-margin products and services, particularly in the Power and Electrification segments.

Business Model Summary

GE Vernova Inc. effectively converts revenue into earnings through a combination of strategic segment focus and operational efficiency. The company’s competitive positioning is reinforced by its diversified portfolio across the energy spectrum, allowing it to capitalize on the growing demand for sustainable energy solutions.

The segment structure reveals a strong emphasis on the Power segment, which not only generates the highest revenue but also shows significant growth potential. The decline in Wind revenue suggests a need for GEV to reassess its strategies in this area, while the growth in Electrification indicates a successful pivot towards more innovative and in-demand solutions.

Notable shifts in the segment mix and margin profile highlight GEV's adaptability in a rapidly changing energy landscape. The increase in gross and operating margins from FY2024 to FY2025 reflects improved operational efficiencies and a focus on higher-margin products. As GEV continues to navigate the evolving energy market, its ability to leverage its strengths in Power and Electrification will be crucial for sustained growth and profitability.
