Revenue Drivers

HSBC Holdings plc operates primarily in the banking and financial services sector, generating revenue through various segments. Over the last three years, total revenue has shown a consistent upward trend, increasing from $15.62 billion in FY2023 to $17.61 billion in FY2025. This growth can be attributed to the performance of its primary business segments, particularly in constant currency revenue, which rose from $7.81 billion in FY2023 to $16.25 billion in FY2025.

The revenue mix has evolved significantly, with the constant currency segment contributing $14.46 billion in FY2024 and $16.25 billion in FY2025, indicating a robust growth trajectory. In contrast, inter-segment revenue has seen a decline from $1.80 billion in FY2024 to $1.36 billion in FY2025. This shift suggests a strategic focus on enhancing core banking operations while reducing reliance on inter-segment transactions.

Key products and services driving growth include retail banking, wealth management, and commercial banking services, with a strong emphasis on digital banking solutions. Geographically, HSBC's operations span multiple regions, with significant contributions from Asia, particularly Hong Kong, which remains a critical market for the bank.
