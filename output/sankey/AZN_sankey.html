# AstraZeneca PLC (AZN) Business Model Analysis

Revenue Drivers

AstraZeneca PLC operates primarily in the pharmaceutical sector, focusing on the development and commercialization of innovative medicines. Over the past three years, the company has demonstrated significant revenue growth, with total revenues increasing from $45.81 billion in FY2023 to $58.74 billion in FY2025, representing a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of approximately 13.5%.

The company's revenue is driven by several key segments, with the following products contributing significantly to total revenue in FY2025:

- Farxiga: $8.40 billion - Tagrisso: $7.25 billion - Imfinzi: $6.06 billion - Ultomiris: $4.72 billion - Calquence: $3.52 billion

These five products alone accounted for approximately 43% of total revenue in FY2025. The revenue mix has evolved notably over the past three years, with Farxiga and Tagrisso showing strong growth trajectories, increasing from $5.96 billion and $5.80 billion in FY2023 to $8.40 billion and $7.25 billion, respectively, in FY2025. Conversely, Soliris has seen a decline in revenue from $3.15 billion in FY2023 to $1.84 billion in FY2025, indicating a shift in the product portfolio and market dynamics.

Geographically, AstraZeneca has a diverse presence, with significant contributions from both established and emerging markets. The growth in revenue can be attributed to the successful launch of new products, expansion in existing markets, and increased demand for oncology and respiratory therapies.

This stability in gross margin reflects the company's ability to maintain pricing power and manage production costs effectively. However, the operating income figures are not disclosed, limiting a full analysis of operating margins.

The major cost drivers for AstraZeneca include research and development (R&D) and selling, general, and administrative (SG&A) expenses. R&D expenses have increased from $10.94 billion in FY2023 to $14.23 billion in FY2025, reflecting the company's commitment to innovation and pipeline development. SG&A expenses have also remained significant, totaling $19.22 billion in FY2023 and $19.93 billion in FY2025.

The increase in R&D spending indicates a focus on long-term growth through new product development, while the relatively stable SG&A expenses suggest effective cost management. The company’s ability to leverage its fixed costs through increased sales has likely contributed to improved profitability trends, although specific operating income figures are not available for analysis.

Business Model Summary

AstraZeneca's business model is centered around converting its substantial revenue into earnings through a combination of high gross margins and strategic investments in R&D. The company’s focus on innovative therapies, particularly in oncology and respiratory diseases, positions it competitively within the pharmaceutical industry.

The segment structure reveals a strong emphasis on high-growth products, with a notable shift in revenue contribution from established products like Soliris to newer therapies such as Farxiga and Tagrisso. This shift not only enhances the revenue mix but also reflects a strategic pivot towards areas with higher growth potential.

Overall, AstraZeneca's ability to maintain high gross margins while investing heavily in R&D underscores its commitment to innovation and long-term growth. The evolving product mix and strong performance of key segments indicate a robust competitive positioning in the pharmaceutical market, with the potential for continued revenue and earnings growth in the coming years.
