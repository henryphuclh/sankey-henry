Revenue Drivers

GE Aerospace operates primarily through two segments: Equipment - Commercial Engines & Services and Services - Defense & Propulsion Technologies. In FY2025, the Equipment segment generated $33.25 billion, accounting for approximately 72.5% of total revenue, while the Services segment contributed $8.87 billion, representing about 19.3% of total revenue. This indicates a strong reliance on commercial engines and services, which has been a consistent trend over the past three years.

In FY2024, total revenue was $38.70 billion, with the Equipment segment at $26.67 billion (69% of total revenue) and Services at $8.03 billion (20.7%). The previous year, FY2023, saw total revenue of $67.95 billion, with Aerospace generating $31.06 billion. The significant drop in total revenue from FY2023 to FY2024 can be attributed to the divestiture of non-core segments, particularly Renewable Energy and Power, which were part of the overall revenue in FY2023.

The revenue mix has evolved, with a clear focus on the Aerospace segment, particularly in commercial engines, which has seen substantial growth. The Equipment segment's revenue increased from $26.67 billion in FY2024 to $33.25 billion in FY2025, reflecting a 24.5% growth year-over-year. This growth is driven by increased demand for commercial aircraft and related services, as the aviation industry continues to recover post-pandemic.

Earnings & Profitability

While gross profit and operating income figures are not disclosed, net income provides insight into profitability trends. In FY2025, GE Aerospace reported a net income of $8.70 billion, up from $6.57 billion in FY2024 and $9.48 billion in FY2023. This indicates a fluctuating profitability trend, with a notable increase in FY2025 despite a decrease in total revenue from FY2023.

The major cost drivers include R&D and SG&A expenses. In FY2025, R&D expenses were $1.58 billion, while SG&A expenses were $4.09 billion. The increase in R&D spending from $1.29 billion in FY2024 to $1.58 billion in FY2025 suggests a commitment to innovation and product development, particularly in the commercial engines segment. SG&A expenses, however, increased from $4.44 billion in FY2024 to $4.09 billion in FY2025, indicating a potential focus on cost management.

Operating leverage appears to be improving, as evidenced by the increase in net income despite fluctuations in total revenue. The growth in the Equipment segment, particularly in commercial engines, is likely contributing to improved margins, although specific margin data is not available.

Business Model Summary

GE Aerospace's business model is centered around converting revenue from its core segments into earnings through a combination of high-value products and services. The Equipment segment, particularly commercial engines, is a significant driver of revenue and profitability, reflecting the company's competitive positioning in the aerospace market.

The segment structure indicates a strategic focus on high-margin products and services, with a clear emphasis on innovation and technological advancement in the aerospace sector. The notable shift in segment mix, moving away from Renewable Energy and Power, highlights GE Aerospace's commitment to strengthening its core competencies in aviation.

Overall, GE Aerospace's ability to convert revenue into earnings is supported by its strong market position in commercial engines and a growing services segment. The company's focus on R&D and cost management will be critical in maintaining profitability and competitive advantage in the evolving aerospace landscape.
