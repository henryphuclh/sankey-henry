Revenue Drivers

NVIDIA Corp operates primarily through several key segments: Compute, Networking, Gaming, Professional Visualization, Automotive, and OEM and Other. In FY2026, the total revenue reached $215.94 billion, with the Compute segment being the largest contributor at $162.36 billion, accounting for approximately 75% of total revenue. The Networking segment followed with $31.38 billion (about 14.5%), while Gaming generated $16.04 billion (around 7.4%). The remaining segments, including Professional Visualization, Automotive, and OEM and Other, contributed smaller amounts, totaling $6.14 billion.

Over the past three years, NVIDIA's revenue mix has evolved significantly. In FY2024, total revenue was $60.92 billion, with Compute & Networking generating $47.41 billion and Graphics contributing $13.52 billion. By FY2025, total revenue increased to $130.50 billion, with a notable rise in the Compute segment to $102.20 billion. The growth trajectory continued into FY2026, where total revenue surged to $215.94 billion, driven primarily by the Compute segment's expansion.

Key products driving this growth include NVIDIA's GPUs, which are essential for gaming, data centers, and AI applications. The company has also seen increased demand for its networking solutions, particularly in cloud computing and enterprise environments, which has contributed to the substantial revenue growth in the Networking segment.

Earnings & Profitability

NVIDIA's profitability metrics reflect strong operational efficiency. In FY2026, the gross margin was 71.1%, while the operating margin stood at 60.4%. The Compute segment, being the largest, also boasts a high gross margin, contributing significantly to overall profitability. The Networking segment, while smaller, has shown a gross margin of approximately 70%, indicating strong pricing power and cost management.

Major cost drivers for NVIDIA include Research and Development (R&D) and Selling, General & Administrative (SG&A) expenses. In FY2026, R&D expenses were $18.50 billion, reflecting the company's commitment to innovation and product development. SG&A expenses were $4.58 billion, which is relatively low compared to total revenue, indicating efficient management of selling and administrative costs.

Operating leverage is evident in NVIDIA's financials, as the company has been able to scale its revenue significantly while maintaining or improving its margins. For instance, from FY2024 to FY2026, total revenue grew from $60.92 billion to $215.94 billion, while operating income increased from $32.97 billion to $130.39 billion, showcasing a robust operating leverage effect.

Business Model Summary

NVIDIA's business model effectively converts revenue into earnings through a combination of high-margin products and efficient cost management. The company's ability to maintain a gross margin above 70% across its segments, particularly in Compute and Networking, highlights its competitive positioning in the technology sector. The significant contribution of the Compute segment to total revenue underscores NVIDIA's leadership in the GPU market, particularly for AI and data center applications.

The segment structure reveals a strategic focus on high-growth areas such as AI, cloud computing, and gaming, which are expected to drive future revenue. Notably, the shift from a more balanced revenue mix in FY2024, where Compute & Networking and Graphics were more evenly distributed, to a dominant Compute segment in FY2026 indicates a strategic pivot towards higher-margin opportunities.

Overall, NVIDIA's business model is characterized by strong revenue growth, high profitability, and a clear focus on innovation, positioning the company well for continued success in the rapidly evolving technology landscape.
