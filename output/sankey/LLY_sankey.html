Revenue Drivers

Eli Lilly and Company has demonstrated significant revenue growth over the past three years, with total revenue increasing from $34.12 billion in FY2023 to $65.18 billion in FY2025. This represents a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of approximately 43.5%. The primary business segments contributing to this growth include cardiometabolic health, oncology, immunology, and collaboration revenues.

In FY2024, the revenue breakdown was as follows:
- Other cardiometabolic health: $11.25 billion
- Collaboration and other revenue: $6.02 billion
- Oncology: $5.21 billion
- Mounjaro®: $3.63 billion
- Immunology: $2.71 billion
- Verzenio®: $2.64 billion
- Trulicity®: $2.19 billion
- Taltz®: $1.55 billion

The revenue mix has evolved significantly, with Mounjaro® emerging as a key growth driver, contributing $3.63 billion in FY2024, up from negligible figures in FY2023. The oncology segment, particularly Verzenio®, has also shown strong performance, with revenues increasing to $2.64 billion in FY2024. The collaboration and other revenue segment has remained a stable contributor, generating $6.02 billion in FY2024.

Earnings & Profitability

While gross profit and operating income figures are not disclosed, net income has shown a robust upward trend, increasing from $5.24 billion in FY2023 to $20.64 billion in FY2025. This growth in net income reflects the company's ability to convert revenue into earnings effectively.

Major cost drivers include Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A) expenses, which amounted to $11.09 billion in FY2025, up from $8.59 billion in FY2024 and $7.40 billion in FY2023. The increase in SG&A expenses indicates a strategic investment in marketing and sales efforts to support the growing product portfolio.

Research and Development (R&D) expenses are not disclosed in the provided data, but they are typically a significant cost for pharmaceutical companies, impacting overall profitability. The operating leverage appears favorable, as the company has managed to increase net income at a faster rate than revenue growth, indicating improved efficiency in converting sales into profits.

Business Model Summary

Eli Lilly's business model is centered around its ability to innovate and bring new products to market, converting revenue into earnings through effective cost management and strategic investments. The company's competitive positioning is evident from its diverse segment structure, which allows it to mitigate risks associated with reliance on a single product or market.

The notable shift in segment mix, particularly the rise of Mounjaro® and the oncology segment, highlights Eli Lilly's focus on high-growth therapeutic areas. The increasing contribution from these segments suggests a strategic pivot towards addressing significant unmet medical needs, which can enhance long-term profitability.

Overall, Eli Lilly's ability to leverage its strong product pipeline, coupled with effective cost management strategies, positions the company favorably within the competitive landscape of the pharmaceutical industry. The growth trajectory observed in recent years underscores the company's commitment to innovation and market expansion, which are critical for sustaining its revenue and earnings growth in the future.
