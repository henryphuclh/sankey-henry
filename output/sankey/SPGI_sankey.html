Revenue Drivers

S&P Global Inc. operates through several key business segments that contribute significantly to its total revenue. For the fiscal year 2025, the company reported total revenue of $15.34 billion, with the following primary segments:

  • Subscription: $7.87 billion (51.3% of total revenue)
  • Non-subscription / Transaction - Ratings: $3.14 billion (20.5%)
  • Non-transaction: $2.05 billion (13.4%)
  • Asset-linked fees: $1.21 billion (7.9%)
  • Sales usage-based royalties: $444 million (2.9%)
  • Recurring variable revenue: $623 million (4.1%)

Over the past three years, the revenue mix has evolved, with the Subscription segment consistently being the largest contributor. In FY2023, Subscription revenue was $1.04 billion, which increased to $7.87 billion by FY2025, indicating a strong growth trajectory. The Non-subscription / Transaction - Ratings segment also showed growth, increasing from $869 million in FY2023 to $3.14 billion in FY2025. This growth reflects S&P Global's focus on expanding its subscription-based services while also capitalizing on transaction-based revenues.

Key products driving growth include the various subscription services, particularly in the areas of Commodity Insights and Mobility, which have shown resilience and growth potential. Geographically, S&P Global's services are utilized globally, with a strong presence in North America and Europe, which are critical markets for its financial and analytical services.

Earnings & Profitability

S&P Global's operating income has shown a positive trend, increasing from $4.02 billion in FY2023 to $6.48 billion in FY2025. The operating margin has also improved, rising from 32.2% in FY2023 to 42.2% in FY2025. This increase in operating margin indicates effective cost management and operational efficiency.

The major cost drivers for S&P Global include Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A) expenses, which were $3.16 billion in FY2023 and increased to $3.42 billion in FY2025. Notably, Research and Development (R&D) expenses are not disclosed, but the company’s focus on innovation in its subscription services likely necessitates ongoing investment in this area.

Operating leverage is evident as revenue growth outpaces the increase in SG&A expenses, contributing to improved profitability. The operating margin percentage increase from 32.2% to 42.2% over the three years highlights the company's ability to convert revenue into earnings effectively.

Business Model Summary

S&P Global converts revenue into earnings primarily through its subscription-based model, which provides a steady and recurring revenue stream. The company’s competitive positioning is strengthened by its diverse segment structure, allowing it to cater to various customer needs across different industries, including financial services, commodities, and analytics.

The notable shift in segment mix is the significant growth in the Subscription segment, which has become the backbone of S&P Global's revenue model. This shift reflects a strategic focus on providing value-added services that enhance customer loyalty and retention. The increase in operating margins also indicates that S&P Global is effectively managing its costs while expanding its revenue base.

In summary, S&P Global Inc. has established a robust business model characterized by a strong subscription revenue base, effective cost management, and a diversified segment structure that positions it well for future growth. The company's ability to adapt and innovate within its segments will be crucial for sustaining its competitive advantage in the financial services industry.
