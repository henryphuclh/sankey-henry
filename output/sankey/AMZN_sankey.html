Revenue Drivers

Amazon.com Inc. operates through several key business segments, each contributing to its overall revenue. For FY2025, the total revenue reached $716.92 billion, with the following primary segments:

  • Online Stores: $269.29 billion (37.5%)
  • Third-party Seller Services: $172.16 billion (24.0%)
  • AWS (Amazon Web Services): $128.72 billion (17.9%)
  • Advertising Services: $68.64 billion (9.6%)
  • Subscription Services: $49.62 billion (6.9%)
  • Physical Stores: $22.56 billion (3.1%)
  • Other: $5.93 billion (0.8%)

Over the past three years, Amazon's revenue mix has evolved significantly. The Online Stores segment has consistently been the largest contributor, growing from $231.87 billion in FY2023 to $269.29 billion in FY2025. Third-party seller services also saw substantial growth, increasing from $140.05 billion to $172.16 billion in the same period. AWS remains a critical growth driver, with revenues rising from $90.76 billion to $128.72 billion.

Geographically, North America remains the largest market, with Q2 2023 revenue of $82.55 billion, showing a steady upward trend. International revenue, while smaller, also demonstrates growth, with Q2 2023 revenue of $29.70 billion.

Earnings & Profitability

Amazon's profitability metrics indicate a robust business model. The gross margin for FY2025 was 50.3%, up from 47.0% in FY2023, reflecting improved efficiency and cost management. The operating margin also improved to 11.2% in FY2025 from 6.4% in FY2023, indicating better operational leverage.

The major cost drivers include Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A) expenses, which totaled $58.30 billion in FY2025, up from $56.19 billion in FY2023. While R&D expenses are not disclosed, the company invests significantly in technology and infrastructure, particularly in AWS and logistics.

The increase in gross and operating margins suggests that Amazon is effectively managing its cost structure while scaling its operations. The growth in higher-margin segments, such as AWS and Advertising Services, contributes positively to overall profitability.

Business Model Summary

Amazon's business model is characterized by its ability to convert revenue into earnings through a diversified segment structure. The company leverages its vast e-commerce platform, cloud computing services, and advertising capabilities to generate substantial revenue streams. The shift towards higher-margin segments, particularly AWS and Advertising Services, enhances its profitability profile.

Competitive positioning is evident in Amazon's extensive ecosystem, which includes a strong logistics network, a vast product selection, and a growing subscription base through services like Amazon Prime. The company’s ability to attract third-party sellers further strengthens its marketplace, providing a competitive edge.

Notably, the segment mix has shifted towards higher-margin services, with AWS and Advertising Services showing significant growth. This shift, combined with improved operational efficiency, positions Amazon favorably for sustained profitability and growth in the future. The company's focus on innovation and customer experience continues to drive its competitive advantage in the e-commerce and cloud computing markets.
