Revenue Drivers

Merck & Co. Inc. operates primarily in the pharmaceutical and animal health sectors, with significant contributions from its key products. Over the past three years, the company has demonstrated robust revenue growth, with total revenues reaching $65.01 billion in FY2025, up from $64.17 billion in FY2024. The revenue mix has evolved, with the pharmaceutical segment accounting for the majority of total revenue, specifically $64.50 billion in FY2025, while the animal health segment contributed $515 million in the same period.

Key products driving growth include Vaxneuvance, Gardasil/Gardasil 9, and Keytruda. For instance, in Q1 2024, Gardasil/Gardasil 9 generated $4.23 billion, while Vaxneuvance contributed $3.31 billion. The international sales of Keytruda also showed promise, with $1.65 billion in Q1 2024. The animal health segment, particularly the Non-US and Companion Animal categories, also contributed significantly, with revenues of $1.17 billion and $608 million, respectively, in Q1 2024.

The revenue mix indicates a strong reliance on the pharmaceutical segment, which has consistently outperformed the animal health segment. This trend highlights Merck's focus on innovative pharmaceutical solutions, particularly in vaccines and oncology.

Earnings & Profitability

While gross profit and operating income figures are not disclosed, net income has shown a positive trajectory, increasing from $17.13 billion in FY2024 to $18.26 billion in FY2025. The major cost drivers for Merck include research and development (R&D) and selling, general, and administrative (SG&A) expenses. R&D expenses were $15.79 billion in FY2025, reflecting the company's commitment to innovation and product development. SG&A expenses were $10.73 billion in the same period, indicating a significant investment in marketing and sales efforts.

The operating leverage appears favorable, as the increase in net income outpaces the growth in expenses. This suggests that Merck is effectively managing its cost structure while driving revenue growth. The company's focus on high-margin products, particularly in the pharmaceutical segment, is likely contributing to improved profitability trends.

Business Model Summary

Merck & Co. Inc. effectively converts revenue into earnings through a combination of innovative product offerings and strategic cost management. The company's business model is centered around high-value pharmaceutical products, which command premium pricing and contribute significantly to overall profitability. The segment structure reveals a competitive positioning that emphasizes the importance of R&D in driving future growth, particularly in oncology and vaccines.

Notable shifts in the segment mix are evident, with a clear focus on expanding the pharmaceutical segment's contribution to total revenue. The strong performance of key products like Gardasil and Keytruda underscores Merck's competitive advantage in the market. Additionally, the animal health segment, while smaller, provides diversification and growth opportunities, particularly in international markets.

Overall, Merck's business model is characterized by a strong emphasis on innovation, effective cost management, and a focus on high-margin products, positioning the company well for sustained growth in the pharmaceutical industry.
