Merck & Co. Inc. (MRK) Business Model Analysis

Revenue Drivers

Merck & Co. Inc. operates primarily through two business segments: Pharmaceuticals and Animal Health. In FY2025, the Pharmaceutical segment generated $58.14 billion, accounting for approximately 89% of total revenue, while the Animal Health segment contributed $6.35 billion, or about 11%. Over the past three years, the revenue from the Pharmaceutical segment has shown a steady increase, rising from $57.54 billion in FY2023 to $58.14 billion in FY2025. Conversely, the Animal Health segment has also seen growth, increasing from $2.57 billion in FY2023 to $6.35 billion in FY2025, indicating a strategic focus on expanding this segment.

The overall revenue for Merck has increased from $60.12 billion in FY2023 to $65.01 billion in FY2025, reflecting a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of approximately 4.1%. This growth can be attributed to strong performance in key products, particularly in the Pharmaceutical segment, which includes blockbuster drugs that have driven sales. The company has also seen a positive trend in international revenues, with Q3 2023 international sales reaching $8.05 billion, up from $7.87 billion in Q2 2023.

Earnings & Profitability

Merck's profitability metrics have shown significant improvement over the past three years. The gross margin has remained robust, with 74.8% in FY2025, slightly down from 76.3% in FY2024 but up from 73.2% in FY2023. The operating margin has also improved, reaching 34.0% in FY2025 compared to 31.5% in FY2024 and a mere 4.9% in FY2023, which was impacted by high R&D expenses.

R&D expenses have been a major cost driver, totaling $15.79 billion in FY2025, down from $17.94 billion in FY2024 and $30.53 billion in FY2023. This reduction in R&D spending, while still significant, suggests a potential shift towards optimizing existing product lines and focusing on profitability. Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A) expenses have remained relatively stable, totaling $10.73 billion in FY2025, compared to $10.82 billion in FY2024 and $10.50 billion in FY2023.

The operating leverage is evident as the company has managed to increase its operating income from $2.95 billion in FY2023 to $22.11 billion in FY2025, showcasing a strong recovery and effective cost management strategies.

Business Model Summary

Merck & Co. Inc. effectively converts revenue into earnings through a combination of high gross margins and controlled operating expenses. The company’s gross margin of 74.8% in FY2025 indicates a strong pricing power and efficient production processes, particularly in its Pharmaceutical segment. The significant increase in operating income reflects the company's ability to scale its operations while managing costs effectively.

The competitive positioning of Merck is evident from its segment structure, with a dominant Pharmaceutical segment that not only drives the majority of revenue but also contributes significantly to profitability. The growth in the Animal Health segment suggests a strategic diversification that could mitigate risks associated with reliance on the Pharmaceutical segment alone.

Notably, the shift in segment mix, with a growing contribution from Animal Health, alongside improvements in margin profiles, indicates a strategic pivot towards a more balanced portfolio. This diversification may enhance resilience against market fluctuations and regulatory challenges in the Pharmaceutical sector.

In summary, Merck & Co. Inc. has demonstrated a robust business model characterized by strong revenue growth, improving profitability, and a strategic focus on both its core Pharmaceutical business and emerging Animal Health segment.
