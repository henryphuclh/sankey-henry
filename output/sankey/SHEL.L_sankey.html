Revenue Drivers

Shell plc operates through several key business segments, which collectively contribute to its total revenue. For the fiscal year 2025, Shell reported total revenue of $266.89 billion, with the following segment contributions:

  • Integrated Gas: $38.46 billion
  • Upstream: $5.11 billion
  • Marketing: $111.86 billion
  • Chemicals and Products: $77.07 billion
  • Renewables and Energy Solutions: $34.36 billion

The revenue mix has evolved over the past three years, with total revenue decreasing from $316.62 billion in FY2023 to $266.89 billion in FY2025. The Marketing segment has been the largest contributor, although it saw a decline from $120.09 billion in FY2024 to $111.86 billion in FY2025. The Chemicals and Products segment also experienced a significant drop from $118.78 billion in FY2023 to $77.07 billion in FY2025. Conversely, Integrated Gas revenue increased slightly from $37.29 billion in FY2024 to $38.46 billion in FY2025, indicating a stable demand in this area.

Key products and services driving growth include natural gas and liquefied natural gas (LNG) in the Integrated Gas segment, while the Marketing segment benefits from fuel sales and retail operations. The Renewables and Energy Solutions segment, although fluctuating, shows potential growth with revenues increasing from $29.37 billion in FY2024 to $34.36 billion in FY2025, reflecting Shell's strategic shift towards sustainable energy solutions.

Earnings & Profitability

While gross profit and operating income figures are not provided, net income for Shell has shown variability, with FY2023 reporting $19.64 billion, FY2024 at $16.52 billion, and FY2025 at $18.12 billion. This indicates a relatively stable profitability trend despite fluctuations in total revenue.

Major cost drivers include:

  • R&D Expense: Increased from $1.10 billion in FY2024 to $1.17 billion in FY2025, reflecting Shell's commitment to innovation, particularly in renewable energy technologies.
  • SG&A Expense: Slightly increased from $12.44 billion in FY2024 to $12.61 billion in FY2025, indicating controlled operational costs.
  • COGS: While specific COGS figures are not provided, the decline in revenue from the Chemicals and Products segment suggests potential pressures on margins in that area.

Operating leverage appears to be a challenge, particularly in the Chemicals and Products segment, which saw a significant revenue drop from $118.78 billion in FY2023 to $77.07 billion in FY2025. This decline may impact overall profitability if fixed costs remain constant.

Business Model Summary

Shell plc's business model is characterized by its ability to convert revenue into earnings through a diversified portfolio of energy-related segments. The company generates revenue primarily from its Marketing and Chemicals segments, which together accounted for approximately 71% of total revenue in FY2025. This diversification allows Shell to mitigate risks associated with fluctuations in any single segment.

The competitive positioning of Shell is evident from its segment structure, with a strong emphasis on both traditional energy sources and a growing focus on renewables. The Integrated Gas segment's stable performance, alongside the growth in Renewables and Energy Solutions, positions Shell favorably in the transition towards a more sustainable energy landscape.

Notable shifts in segment mix include the decline in the Chemicals and Products segment, which may indicate a need for strategic reassessment in that area. Additionally, the increase in Integrated Gas and Renewables suggests a strategic pivot towards more sustainable and stable revenue sources, aligning with global energy trends.

In summary, Shell's business model reflects a complex interplay of traditional and emerging energy markets, with a focus on maintaining profitability amidst evolving market dynamics. The company's ability to adapt its segment focus will be crucial in navigating future challenges and opportunities in the energy sector.
