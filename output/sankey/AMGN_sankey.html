Revenue Drivers

Amgen Inc. operates primarily in the biotechnology sector, focusing on the development and commercialization of innovative therapies. Over the past three years, the company has demonstrated significant revenue growth, with total revenues increasing from $28.19 billion in FY2023 to $36.75 billion in FY2025, reflecting a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of approximately 14.8%.

The company's revenue is derived from several key segments, with the largest contributors being "Other products," Prolia, and Repatha. In FY2025, "Other products" generated $7.26 billion, accounting for approximately 19.8% of total revenue. Prolia and Repatha followed, contributing $4.41 billion (12.0%) and $3.02 billion (8.2%), respectively. The revenue mix has evolved, with Prolia showing consistent growth from $4.05 billion in FY2023 to $4.41 billion in FY2025, while Repatha's revenue surged from $1.64 billion to $3.02 billion in the same period, indicating strong market demand and effective sales strategies.

Other notable products include Otezla, ENBREL, and XGEVA, which also contributed significantly to revenue. Otezla's revenue increased from $2.19 billion in FY2023 to $2.27 billion in FY2025, while ENBREL's revenue declined from $3.70 billion to $2.23 billion, reflecting potential market saturation or increased competition. The overall revenue mix indicates a shift towards newer products like Prolia and Repatha, which are driving growth.

Earnings & Profitability

Amgen's operating income has shown resilience, increasing from $7.90 billion in FY2023 to $9.08 billion in FY2025, with operating margins fluctuating between 21.7% and 28.0% over the same period. The operating margin for FY2025 stands at 24.7%, indicating effective cost management and operational efficiency.

The major cost drivers for Amgen include Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A) expenses and Research and Development (R&D) expenses. SG&A expenses have remained relatively stable, increasing from $6.18 billion in FY2023 to $7.05 billion in FY2025. This suggests a focus on maintaining sales force effectiveness and marketing efforts to support product launches and market penetration. However, specific R&D expenses were not disclosed, which limits the analysis of investment in innovation.

Operating leverage is evident as revenue growth outpaces the increase in SG&A expenses, contributing to improved profitability. The company has effectively managed its cost structure, allowing for a higher proportion of revenue to flow through to operating income.

Business Model Summary

Amgen's business model is centered around converting its substantial revenue into earnings through a combination of innovative product offerings and efficient operational practices. The company leverages its strong pipeline of biologics and biosimilars to maintain a competitive edge in the biotechnology market. The segment structure reveals a diversified portfolio, with a mix of established products and newer therapies that cater to various therapeutic areas.

The notable shift in segment mix, particularly the growth of Prolia and Repatha, highlights Amgen's strategic focus on high-demand therapies that address significant unmet medical needs. This shift, coupled with a declining contribution from ENBREL, suggests a proactive approach to adapt to market dynamics and evolving healthcare demands.

Overall, Amgen's ability to maintain strong operating margins while expanding its revenue base underscores its competitive positioning in the biotechnology sector. The company's focus on innovation, coupled with effective cost management, positions it well for sustained growth in the coming years.
