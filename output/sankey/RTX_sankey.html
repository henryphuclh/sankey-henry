Revenue Drivers

RTX Corporation operates through three primary business segments: Collins Aerospace Systems, Pratt and Whitney, and Raytheon, with a minor contribution from the "Other" segment. In FY2025, the total revenue reached $88.60 billion, with the following contributions from each segment:

  • Collins Aerospace Systems: $27.59 billion
  • Pratt and Whitney: $32.92 billion
  • Raytheon: $27.89 billion
  • Other: $210 million

Over the past three years, RTX has experienced significant revenue growth, increasing from $68.92 billion in FY2023 to $80.74 billion in FY2024, and further to $88.60 billion in FY2025. The revenue mix has evolved, with Pratt and Whitney showing the most substantial growth, from $18.30 billion in FY2023 to $32.92 billion in FY2025, indicating a strong demand for its aerospace engines and services. Collins Aerospace Systems and Raytheon also contributed positively, with revenues of $26.25 billion and $26.35 billion in FY2023, respectively, growing to $27.59 billion and $27.89 billion by FY2025.

Key products driving growth include advanced aerospace systems, military and commercial engines, and defense technologies. The geographical diversification of RTX's operations allows it to tap into various markets, enhancing its revenue stability and growth potential.

Earnings & Profitability

RTX's operating income has shown a positive trend, increasing from $3.56 billion in FY2023 to $6.54 billion in FY2024, and reaching $9.30 billion in FY2025. The operating margin has also improved, from 5.2% in FY2023 to 10.5% in FY2025. This improvement reflects effective cost management and operational efficiencies across its segments.

While gross profit figures are not provided, the operating margin percentages suggest that RTX is effectively converting revenue into earnings. The major cost drivers include Research and Development (R&D) and Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A) expenses. R&D expenses have remained relatively stable, with $2.81 billion in FY2023, increasing slightly to $2.93 billion in FY2024, and then decreasing to $2.81 billion in FY2025. SG&A expenses have also shown a slight increase from $5.81 billion in FY2023 and FY2024 to $6.09 billion in FY2025.

The operating leverage is evident as RTX scales its operations, leading to improved profitability. The increase in operating income relative to revenue growth indicates that the company is managing its costs effectively while expanding its top line.

Business Model Summary

RTX Corporation's business model is centered around converting its substantial revenue into earnings through a diversified portfolio of aerospace and defense products and services. The company has demonstrated a strong ability to generate operating income, with a notable increase in operating margins from 5.2% in FY2023 to 10.5% in FY2025. This conversion of revenue into earnings is supported by a focus on high-margin segments, particularly Pratt and Whitney, which has seen significant revenue growth and contributes the largest share to total revenue.

The competitive positioning of RTX is reinforced by its segment structure, which allows it to leverage synergies across its aerospace and defense operations. The growth in Pratt and Whitney's revenue indicates a strong competitive advantage in the aerospace engine market, while Collins Aerospace Systems and Raytheon provide complementary capabilities in aerospace systems and defense technologies.

Notable shifts in the segment mix include the increasing dominance of Pratt and Whitney, which has nearly doubled its revenue from FY2023 to FY2025. This shift, along with the overall improvement in operating margins, highlights RTX's strategic focus on high-growth areas within the aerospace and defense sectors, positioning the company for continued success in a competitive landscape.
