# Toronto Dominion Bank (TD.TO) Business Model Analysis

Revenue Drivers

Toronto Dominion Bank (TD) operates through several key business segments, which collectively contribute to its total revenue. For FY2025, TD reported total revenue of $67.78 billion, with the following segment contributions:

- Canadian Personal and Commercial Banking: $20.69 billion (30.5%) - U.S. Retail: $12.30 billion (18.1%) - Wealth Management and Insurance: $14.56 billion (21.5%) - Wholesale Banking: $8.39 billion (12.4%) - Corporate: $11.83 billion (17.4%)

Over the past three years, TD's revenue mix has evolved significantly. In FY2023, total revenue was $50.49 billion, with the Canadian Personal and Commercial Banking segment generating $18.32 billion (36.3%) and U.S. Retail contributing $14.44 billion (28.6%). By FY2024, total revenue increased to $57.22 billion, with notable growth in the Corporate segment, which rose from $635 million in FY2023 to $2.90 billion in FY2024.

The growth in the Corporate segment continued into FY2025, reaching $11.83 billion, indicating a strategic shift or expansion in this area. The Wealth Management and Insurance segment also showed strong growth, increasing from $11.28 billion in FY2023 to $14.56 billion in FY2025. This suggests a focus on diversifying revenue sources and enhancing service offerings in wealth management.

Key products and services driving growth include personal banking services in Canada, retail banking in the U.S., and wealth management solutions, which have become increasingly important as consumer preferences shift towards comprehensive financial services.

Toronto Dominion Bank effectively converts revenue into earnings through a diversified business model that capitalizes on its strong presence in both Canadian and U.S. markets. The bank's ability to generate significant net income, particularly in FY2025, demonstrates its operational efficiency and strategic focus on high-growth segments.
