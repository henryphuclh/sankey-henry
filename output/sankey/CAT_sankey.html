Revenue Drivers

Caterpillar Inc. (CAT) operates through several key business segments, which are critical to its overall revenue generation. For the fiscal year 2025, the company reported total revenue of $67.59 billion, with the following contributions from its primary segments:

  • Construction Industries: $25.06 billion (37%)
  • Resource Industries: $12.47 billion (18%)
  • Power & Energy: $32.10 billion (47%)
  • Financial Products Segment: $4.22 billion (6%)

Over the past three years, the revenue mix has shown some shifts. In FY2023, the Energy & Transportation segment (now reported as Power & Energy) generated $28.00 billion, while the Construction Industries segment contributed $27.42 billion. By FY2025, the Power & Energy segment saw a significant increase to $32.10 billion, indicating a strategic focus on energy solutions. The Construction Industries segment, however, declined slightly to $25.06 billion in FY2025 from $27.42 billion in FY2023.

Key products driving growth include heavy machinery and equipment for construction and mining, as well as energy solutions that cater to various industries. Geographically, Caterpillar has a strong presence in North America, which remains a significant market, but it is also expanding its footprint in emerging markets.

Earnings & Profitability

Caterpillar's profitability is reflected in its gross and operating margins. For FY2025, the company reported a gross margin of 33.8% and an operating margin of 16.5%. The gross margin has fluctuated over the past three years, peaking at 38.0% in FY2024, which indicates effective cost management and pricing strategies during that period.

The major cost drivers for Caterpillar include:

  • Research and Development (R&D): $2.15 billion in FY2025, which is essential for innovation and maintaining competitive advantage.
  • Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A): $6.99 billion in FY2025, reflecting the costs associated with marketing, sales, and administrative functions.
  • Cost of Goods Sold (COGS): While specific COGS figures are not provided, the gross profit of $22.84 billion in FY2025 suggests that COGS is a significant component of total expenses.

Operating leverage is evident as the company has managed to maintain relatively stable operating income despite fluctuations in revenue. The operating income for FY2025 was $11.15 billion, down from $13.07 billion in FY2024, indicating that while revenues have slightly decreased, the company has maintained a strong operational framework.
