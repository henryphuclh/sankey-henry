Booking Holdings Inc. (BKNG) Business Model Analysis

Revenue Drivers

Booking Holdings Inc. operates primarily through three business segments: Merchant revenues, Agency revenues, and Advertising and other revenues. In FY2025, the company reported total revenue of $26.92 billion, with Merchant revenues contributing the largest share at $17.75 billion (66% of total revenue). Agency revenues followed with $7.97 billion (30%), while Advertising and other revenues accounted for $1.19 billion (4%).

Over the past three years, the revenue mix has shown a notable shift. In FY2023, Merchant revenues were $10.94 billion, representing 51% of total revenue, while Agency revenues were slightly higher at $9.41 billion (44%). By FY2024, Merchant revenues increased to $14.14 billion (60%), while Agency revenues decreased to $8.52 billion (36%). This trend continued into FY2025, where Merchant revenues further increased, solidifying their dominance in the revenue mix.

The growth in Merchant revenues can be attributed to the company's focus on direct bookings through its platforms, which typically yield higher margins compared to agency bookings. The decline in Agency revenues suggests a strategic shift towards enhancing the Merchant model, which is likely to drive future growth. Additionally, the Advertising and other revenues segment has shown steady growth, increasing from $1.01 billion in FY2023 to $1.19 billion in FY2025, indicating a successful expansion of ancillary services.

Earnings & Profitability

Booking Holdings has demonstrated strong profitability, with operating income increasing from $5.83 billion in FY2023 to $8.82 billion in FY2025. The operating margin has also improved, rising from 27.3% in FY2023 to 32.8% in FY2025. This increase in operating margin reflects the company's ability to manage costs effectively while scaling its revenue.

While gross profit figures are not disclosed, the operating margin by segment can provide insights into profitability. The Merchant segment, being the largest, likely has a higher operating margin compared to Agency revenues, which typically involve lower margins due to commission structures. The operating margin for the Agency segment has decreased from FY2023 to FY2025, indicating potential challenges in this area, while Merchant revenues have benefitted from economies of scale.

Major cost drivers for Booking Holdings include Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A) expenses, which are essential for marketing and customer acquisition, and technology investments that enhance the user experience. The company has not disclosed specific R&D expenses, but investments in technology are critical for maintaining competitive advantages in the online travel market.

Business Model Summary

Booking Holdings converts revenue into earnings primarily through its focus on direct bookings, which yield higher margins compared to traditional agency models. The company's competitive positioning is evident from its segment structure, with a clear emphasis on growing Merchant revenues, which have consistently outperformed Agency revenues in terms of growth and profitability.

The notable shift in segment mix, with Merchant revenues increasing significantly over the past three years, highlights the company's strategic pivot towards a more profitable business model. This shift is supported by a robust operating margin improvement, indicating effective cost management and operational efficiency.

In summary, Booking Holdings Inc. has successfully leveraged its business model to enhance profitability through a focus on direct bookings and ancillary services, positioning itself favorably in the competitive online travel market. The evolving revenue mix and improving margins suggest a strong foundation for future growth.
