Revenue Drivers

Verizon Communications operates primarily through two business segments: Consumer and Business. In FY2025, the total revenue reached $138.19 billion, with the Operating Segments Excluding Intersegment Elimination contributing $80.62 billion. The Business segment generated $29.07 billion, which includes $25.40 billion from service revenues and other business activities, and $3.70 billion from wireless equipment sales.

Over the past three years, Verizon's revenue mix has shown a slight shift. In FY2023, the Business segment accounted for 30.12 billion, which decreased to $29.53 billion in FY2024 and further to $29.07 billion in FY2025. This decline in the Business segment's revenue is notable, especially as the overall revenue increased from $133.97 billion in FY2023 to $138.19 billion in FY2025. The Consumer segment, while not explicitly detailed in the annual reports, can be inferred to have remained a significant contributor, with quarterly revenues in 2024 averaging around $25 billion.

Key products driving growth include wireless services and equipment, which remain essential in both consumer and business markets. The company’s focus on expanding its 5G network and enhancing service offerings has likely contributed to maintaining a robust customer base.

Earnings & Profitability

Verizon's operating income has shown a positive trend, increasing from $22.88 billion in FY2023 to $29.26 billion in FY2025, reflecting an operating margin improvement from 17.1% to 21.2%. The Business segment's operating margin is not explicitly provided, but the overall operating margin indicates effective cost management and operational efficiency.

The major cost drivers for Verizon include Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A) expenses, which totaled $33.82 billion in FY2025, up from $32.74 billion in FY2023. The increase in SG&A expenses, despite rising revenues, suggests that the company is investing in customer acquisition and retention strategies. Notably, R&D expenses are not disclosed, but the focus on technological advancements, particularly in 5G, implies ongoing investment in innovation.

Operating leverage appears to be improving, as evidenced by the rising operating income relative to revenue growth. The operating margin has increased slightly over the years, indicating that Verizon is effectively converting a larger portion of its revenue into profit.

Business Model Summary

Verizon Communications converts revenue into earnings primarily through its extensive telecommunications infrastructure and service offerings. The company’s ability to maintain a high operating margin, which reached 21.2% in FY2025, demonstrates its efficiency in managing costs relative to revenue generation.

The competitive positioning of Verizon is evident from its segment structure. The dual focus on Consumer and Business segments allows for diversified revenue streams, reducing reliance on any single market. The Business segment, while experiencing a slight decline in revenue, remains a critical part of the overall strategy, particularly in providing enterprise solutions and services.

Notable shifts in the segment mix include the increasing contribution of the Operating Segments Excluding Intersegment Elimination, which rose from $74.87 billion in FY2023 to $80.62 billion in FY2025. This growth reflects Verizon's strategic emphasis on enhancing its core telecommunications services while managing intersegment eliminations effectively.

In summary, Verizon's business model is characterized by a strong focus on operational efficiency, a diversified revenue base, and a commitment to technological advancement, positioning the company well for future growth in the competitive telecommunications landscape.
