Revenue Drivers

Linde plc operates primarily in the industrial gases sector, with its revenue generated from five key segments: Americas, EMEA (Europe, Middle East, and Africa), APAC (Asia-Pacific), Engineering, and Other. For FY2025, total revenue reached $33.99 billion, with the Americas segment contributing the largest share at $15.21 billion, representing approximately 44.8% of total revenue. The EMEA segment followed with $8.55 billion (25.2%), APAC with $6.66 billion (19.6%), Engineering at $2.25 billion (6.6%), and Other at $1.32 billion (3.9%).

Over the past three years, Linde has demonstrated a consistent upward trend in total revenue, increasing from $32.85 billion in FY2023 to $33.99 billion in FY2025. The Americas segment has shown the most significant growth, rising from $14.30 billion in FY2023 to $15.21 billion in FY2025. The EMEA segment also experienced growth, albeit at a slower pace, while APAC maintained stable revenue levels. The Engineering segment saw a slight decline in FY2025 compared to FY2024, indicating potential challenges in that area. The revenue mix has evolved, with the Americas segment becoming increasingly dominant, reflecting Linde's strategic focus on this region.

Key products and services driving growth include industrial gases used in various applications such as healthcare, manufacturing, and energy. The geographical diversification of Linde's operations allows it to capitalize on growth opportunities in emerging markets, particularly in the Americas and APAC regions.

Earnings & Profitability

Linde's operating income for FY2025 was $8.92 billion, resulting in an operating margin of 26.3%, a slight increase from 26.2% in FY2024 and 24.4% in FY2023. This upward trend in operating margin indicates improved efficiency and cost management across the business. While gross profit figures are not disclosed, the operating income suggests that Linde effectively converts a significant portion of its revenue into earnings.

Major cost drivers for Linde include Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A) expenses and Research and Development (R&D) expenses. In FY2025, SG&A expenses amounted to $3.43 billion, while R&D expenses were $147 million. The relatively low R&D expenditure indicates a focus on operational efficiency rather than extensive product development, which is typical in the industrial gases sector. The operating leverage is evident as Linde has managed to increase its operating income while maintaining a stable cost structure, contributing to improved profitability trends.

Business Model Summary

Linde plc's business model is centered around converting revenue from its diverse industrial gases portfolio into earnings through efficient operations and strategic market positioning. The company leverages its extensive global footprint to serve a wide range of industries, ensuring a steady demand for its products and services.

The competitive positioning of Linde is reinforced by its strong presence in the Americas, which has become the largest revenue contributor. This segment's growth reflects Linde's ability to capitalize on market opportunities and its established customer base. The segment structure also highlights Linde's focus on high-margin areas, particularly in the Americas and EMEA, while managing costs effectively.

This shift indicates a strategic emphasis on regions with higher growth potential. Additionally, the slight decline in the Engineering segment's revenue suggests a need for Linde to reassess its strategies in this area to maintain overall growth and profitability.

In summary, Linde plc's business model is characterized by a strong revenue-generating capability across its segments, effective cost management, and a strategic focus on high-growth regions, positioning the company well for future growth.
